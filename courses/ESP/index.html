Engineering Strategies & Practice

Many first-year engineering students struggle with APS111/112. I'll try my best to explain everything in this page. APS112 is up, I will post APS111 later in 2025.

APS112

Congratulations, you’ve gotten APS111 out of the way! The start of APS112 will be deja vu for you, as you will have another Team Charter (7%) and Project Requirements (10%) doc to do with a new team (there may be some added requirements for the TC, I don’t remember completely). However, there are a few new things I would like to bring up (and not the lack of mandatory Perusall readings).

The Client

The biggest added “RNG factor” in this course is your client. They may suck at writing a client statement and give you unnecessary info. You may find your friend got a project you wish you would have done instead1. The client statement may confuse you at first.

You will need to set up three client meetings during the semester. All client communication must be approved by the Engineering Manager (EM)2 before being sent, including your intended questions during meetings. In-person meetings are preferred unless the client is located outside Greater Toronto or the client is unable to do one. One of my friends had to go to Markham for client meetings. Your engineering notebook will be very important here as you generally can not record meetings so your notes will be the only record of what happened.

The first meeting will be yours to ask questions that will help you with writing your Project Requirements (particularly if the client statement was vague). The second will be a review of the Project Requirements. The client may ask you to adjust the FOCs as they see fit. For example, my client requested we lower our budget from $5,000 to $1,500 for the project3. The third will be a review of the CDS, similar to that of the Project Requirements.

Your client may also show up to the final presentation and ask questions. The CI and EM present may factor that into your final presentation grade. I will discuss the final presentation further below.

Status Reports

Each of your six team members will need to write a status report and send it to your EM at some point during the semester. These are not graded, but rather are one of the factors your EM will look at when determining your Performance Review grade. There is a template for it and there are two parts. Part 1 must be submitted on MS Teams before your tutorial (or earlier, please ask your EM for clarification if you’re not sure) and contains your team’s agenda for that tutorial, current tasks, issues the team encountered since the last status report and how it dealt with them, and decisions the team made since the last status report. You must also submit relevant parts of your team’s Gantt Chart for Part A. Following your tutorial, the person responsible for that week’s status report must submit a table of upcoming tasks within 24 hours.

The Preliminary Literature Review (PLR)

The Preliminary Literature Review is an individual assignment worth 5% of your final mark. It’s a simple worksheet where you do the following:
-Introduce and state your research question
-Link to and cite your source using proper IEEE formatting
-Perform the CRAAP test on your source
-Explain the relationship between your source and the project.

Team Role Observation & Analysis (TROA)

This 10% individual assignment is similar to your Teamwork Analysis, but with some key differences. The situation you choose must show how you fit your role in the team in that situation. Like in the Teamwork Analysis, you will analyze whether the team charter helped and suggest changes to it in your conclusion. However, if you do not think you fit this role you may instead suggest a different role which would be better suited for you.

This was my worst assignment in APS112.

The Midterm

This will likely be your last midterm. In 2024, it was held the day after the CDS deadline and it will be the only exam in the course4. Two-thirds of the multiple-choice questions will be similar in style to the APS111 exam. There were one or two questions about estimation, and the rest involved Gantt Charts (i.e. scheduling). The midterm is worth 15% of your final grade and the median grade for the 2024 midterm was 60%.

Design Review Gateway (DRG)

In mid-March (around 2 weeks before the CDS is due), your team will present its progress on the project, including an overview of the project, changes made during your second client meeting, any challenges your team has faced, and the current status of the project. The point of this presentation is to convince your EM and CI that your project is going well. If you are a design engineer, a failing or below grade would indicate your boss may pull the plug on your project. For this reason, it is crucial to not procrastinate too much on your CDS. You will have an 8-minute time limit (you may be cut off at that time) and the CDS and EM will ask you questions after. Hostile questions are possible.

This presentation is worth 5% of your final mark, the weighting of which will be moved to your final presentation if you do better on it. If you would like to learn more about presentation skills, I can’t remember them right now but I have some material to help. Please contact me if you’d like it.

The CDS

In 112, the CDS will be similar to that in 111 but will also contain your measures of success. These will be more detailed tests of your solution and may involve prototyping or running a simulation. That doesn’t mean you’ll need it to be that advanced, but at least make some kind of model. In the CDS, you will talk about the measures of success you plan on doing. After you are done your CDS, you will perform these tests in preparation for your final presentation.

Like in APS111, the CDS is worth 20% of your final mark.

The Final Presentation

Your team’s 20% final presentation is the “final exam” of ESP, held any time between 9 and 6 on the first day of exams. The table of rooms and times will be posted in advance. Like the DRG, you will have a time limit (this one is 12 minutes). You will be cut off at the limit whether or not you were able to finish. The point of this presentation is to convince your client to implement your design solution or one of your alternatives.

After your presentation, you can be questioned by the CI, EM, the client (if they come), or anyone else in the room. The CI and EM are not the same CI and EM you had throughout the semester, and your client may be the EM if they are part of the teaching team. You are allowed to visit other people’s presentations as long as you aren’t doing your own at that time. In my section, the CI gave two of the three groups who presented hostile questions (my group was spared).

The final presentation is worth 20% of your final mark, though if you do better in it than your DRG, the DRG weight will be redistributed to your final presentation.
