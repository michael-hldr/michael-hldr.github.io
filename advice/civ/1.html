Moments (CIV100 & CIV102)

Try opening a door. Push it at a point far from the hinge, then try pushing it near the hinge. It was probably significantly harder to open near the hinge. This can be explained by moments, which you can think of as a rotational force. When you open the door, you are exerting a moment rotating about the hinge of the door. The formula for moment is \(M = F\,d\,\sin(\theta)\), where \(d\) is the distance between your force \(F\) and your hinge, and \(\theta\) is the angle between your force and the line between it and your hinge, as shown below.
