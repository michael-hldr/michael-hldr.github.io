Vectors & Resultant forces (High School Review for CIV100 & CIV102)

If you are comfortable with vector addition, you can skip to the next section.

There are two methods of vector addition: law of cosines and using components. Law of cosines treats the two vectors, positioned head to tail, and the resultant as three edges of a triangle as shown below. If you have the magnitudes of each vector and the angle \(\theta\), you can use cosine law to find the magnitude of the resultant. Using components, you split the vectors into each of their x and y components and add each of them together to find the x and y components, as shown below.


A couple quick practice problems. These are very easy and should just be a confirmation of your understanding:
1. If \(\vec v_1\) and \(\vec v_2 \) are attached tail-to-tail with an angle of \(30\) degrees and magnitudes of \(3\) and \(5\), respectively, what is the magnitude of \(\vec v_1 + \vec v_2\)? What is the angle between \(\vec v_1 + \vec v_2\) and \(\vec v_1\) when the two vectors are placed tail-to-tail?
2. If \(\vec v_1 = (1, 2)\), \(\vec v_2 = (4, -3)\), and \(\vec v_3 = (-1, 6)\), find \(\vec v_1 + 6\vec v_2 - 3\vec v_3\).
